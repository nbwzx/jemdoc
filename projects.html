Past Projects

Khovanov homology and knot concordance

Khovanov homology is an oriented link invariant that arises as a homology of a chain complex. It can be regarded as a categorification of the Jones polynomial. The slice genus is the minimum genus of a smooth surface embedded in the 4-ball whose boundary is the knot. In general, the slice genus is difficult to compute. However, we can get a lower bound from the Rasmussen invariant. And we can see its wide applications in calculating the slice genus of some knots.

A Generalized Determinant of Matrices and Applications

With Xuesong Lu, Songtao Mao and Yuehui Zhang

A generalized definition of the determinant of matrices is given, which is compatible with the usual determinant for square matrices and keeps many important properties, such as being an alternating multilinear function, keeping multiplication formula and partly keeping the Cauchy-Binet's formula. As applications of the new theory, the generalized Cramer's rule and the generalized oriented volume are obtained.

A Short Review of 'EIGENVECTORS FROM EIGENVALUES'

In this paper, I give an example and some comparisons between the new method in 'EIGENVECTORS FROM EIGENVALUES' and the original method to calculate the eigenvectors of a Hermitian matrix.

Birkhoff's Problem with All Proofs

With Qi Zhou

By giving the permutation representation on the finite dimensional space, we discovered a basis for generating all possible matrices. Then, we researched the space spanned by n×n permutation matrices and finally proved Birkhoff's Theorem.

Methods of Primality Testing

In this paper, I listed some historically typical algorithms for primality testing and evaluated their pros and cons, including trial division algorithm, Fermat primality test, Lucas primality test, Solovay–Strassen primality test and AKS primality test.
